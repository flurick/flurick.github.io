Reasons of bad open software

Some sort of summary of Vannevar symposium 1995.

If you sell closed sourced programs, only you can fix it. If you make open sourced progamms, you trust the user to fix it. if you do not trust your user to work on it, you might grow to dislike them to the point of not fixing it yourself. It takes some will power to work on your own projects, and even the slightest possibility of you not having to do it your self reduces the possisbility of you getting anything done, since you might not have to.

Getting into the mind set that it's all on you might work for a while, until you have given yuor everyting expecting something in return. Surprise! It's a process not a project, there is no end unless you say so, but you wont since you are a good person and heroes do not leave things half done, right?
